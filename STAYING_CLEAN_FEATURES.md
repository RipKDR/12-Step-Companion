# Features to Help Users Stay Clean: Evidence-Based Relapse Prevention

## Research Foundation

**Key Statistics**:
- 40-60% of people in recovery experience relapse (similar to other chronic diseases)
- Relapse is a gradual process with 3 stages: Emotional ‚Üí Mental ‚Üí Physical
- Early intervention is critical - catching relapse in emotional/mental stages prevents physical relapse

**Evidence-Based Approaches**:
- Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) for Relapse Prevention
- Mindfulness-Based Relapse Prevention (MBRP)
- If-Then Planning (Implementation Intentions)
- Trigger Identification & Avoidance
- Coping Skills Development

## üéØ Category 1: Early Warning System & Relapse Detection

### 1. **Relapse Warning Signs Tracker** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê CRITICAL
**Research**: Relapse happens in 3 stages - catching it early prevents physical relapse

**How It Works**:
- **Emotional Relapse Signs** (track daily):
  - Bottling up emotions
  - Isolation from others
  - Poor eating habits
  - Poor sleep patterns
  - Skipping meetings
  - Not asking for help
- **Mental Relapse Signs** (track when they occur):
  - Cravings for drugs/alcohol
  - Nostalgic feelings about using
  - Lying to self about consequences
  - Bargaining ("just one time")
  - Thinking about people/places/things associated with use
- **Physical Relapse Signs** (immediate intervention):
  - Planning to use
  - Seeking out substances
  - Actual use

**Implementation**:
- Daily check-in: "How are you feeling?" (emotional signs)
- Weekly assessment: "Any cravings or thoughts about using?" (mental signs)
- Risk score calculation based on signs
- Automatic alerts when risk increases
- Sponsor notification (with consent) when risk is high
- Action plan automatically suggested when signs detected

**Why It Works**: Early detection allows intervention before physical relapse occurs.

---

### 2. **Risk Score & Predictive Analytics** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê CRITICAL
**Research**: Patterns predict relapse risk - early intervention prevents relapse

**How It Works**:
- Calculate risk score based on:
  - Warning signs present
  - Recent behavior changes (missed meetings, entries, routines)
  - Trigger exposure
  - Stress levels
  - Sleep quality
  - Social support engagement
- Risk levels: Low (0-30), Moderate (31-60), High (61-80), Critical (81-100)
- Visual risk indicator (traffic light: green/yellow/red)
- Trend analysis: "Your risk has increased 20% this week"

**Implementation**:
- Daily risk calculation
- Weekly trend analysis
- Alerts when risk increases significantly
- Personalized recommendations based on risk factors
- Sponsor dashboard shows risk level (with consent)

**Why It Works**: Awareness of risk level motivates preventive action.

---

### 3. **Pattern Recognition & Predictive Alerts** ‚≠ê‚≠ê HIGH IMPACT
**Research**: Relapse often follows predictable patterns

**How It Works**:
- Analyze historical data to identify patterns:
  - "You typically struggle on Fridays"
  - "Your risk increases when you skip 3+ meetings"
  - "Stress levels above 7 correlate with cravings"
  - "Poor sleep (under 6 hours) increases risk"
- Proactive alerts: "Based on your patterns, this week might be challenging"
- Personalized recommendations: "Here's your Action Plan for Friday"

**Implementation**:
- Machine learning analysis of user patterns
- Pattern identification after 30+ days of data
- Proactive alerts before high-risk periods
- Personalized recommendations
- Pattern updates as more data is collected

**Why It Works**: Forewarned is forearmed - knowing when risk is high allows preparation.

## üéØ Category 2: Trigger Management & Avoidance

### 4. **Comprehensive Trigger Identification** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê CRITICAL
**Research**: Identifying triggers is the first step in relapse prevention (CBT)

**How It Works**:
- **Internal Triggers** (track and identify):
  - Emotions: Depression, anxiety, stress, anger, boredom, loneliness
  - Physical: Fatigue, hunger, pain, withdrawal symptoms
  - Mental: Negative thoughts, self-doubt, memories
- **External Triggers** (track and identify):
  - People: Old using friends, family conflicts
  - Places: Bars, old using locations, certain neighborhoods
  - Things: Paraphernalia, smells, sounds, certain times of day
  - Situations: Parties, celebrations, stress at work, relationship issues

**Implementation**:
- Trigger identification questionnaire (onboarding + periodic updates)
- Trigger log: "What triggered this craving?"
- Trigger database: Build personal trigger list over time
- Trigger patterns: "You're most triggered by stress and loneliness"
- Avoidance strategies: "Here's how to avoid this trigger"

**Why It Works**: You can't avoid what you don't know - awareness is the first step.

---

### 5. **Geofenced Trigger Locations** ‚≠ê‚≠ê HIGH IMPACT (Already Planned - Enhance)
**Research**: Environmental cues are powerful triggers

**How It Works** (Enhanced):
- Set trigger locations with radius
- **On Enter**:

  - "You're near a trigger location - here's your plan"
- **On Exit**:

  - Log success or challenges
  - Update Action Plan based on experience
- **Smart Suggestions**:
  - "You've been near this location 3 times this week - consider avoiding it"
  - "You successfully handled this location - great job!"

**Implementation**:
- Background geofencing (already planned)
- Automatic Action Plan opening
- Sponsor notifications (opt-in)
- Success tracking and reinforcement

**Why It Works**: Just-in-time intervention at the moment of trigger exposure.

### 6. **Trigger Avoidance Strategies** ‚≠ê‚≠ê HIGH IMPACT
**Research**: Avoidance is the first line of defense (Recognize, Avoid, Cope)

**How It Works**:
- For each identified trigger, develop avoidance strategies:
  - **People**: "Avoid John - he's a trigger. Instead, call your sponsor."
  - **Places**: "Don't go to that bar. Here are 3 alternative locations."
  - **Situations**: "If invited to a party, bring a sober friend."
- Pre-planned avoidance strategies stored in Action Plans
- Reminders: "You have a trigger situation coming up - here's your plan"

**Implementation**:
- Link triggers to Action Plans
- Avoidance strategy library
- Pre-event reminders
- Success tracking: "You avoided 5 triggers this week"

**Why It Works**: Having a plan reduces decision-making in high-risk situations.

**How It Works** (Enhanced):

- **Action Plan Library**: Pre-built plans for common situations

**Implementation**:
- If-Then plan builder (already planned - enhance with templates)

**Why It Works**: Research shows If-Then planning increases follow-through by 2-3x.

**Implementation**:

- Quick access during cravings

**Why It Works**: Visualizing consequences reduces impulsive decisions.

## üéØ Category 8: Relapse Response & Recovery

### 22. **Lapse vs. Relapse Response** ‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê CRITICAL
**Research**: How you respond to a lapse determines if it becomes a relapse

**How It Works**:
- **Lapse Response** (if use occurs):
  - "You had a lapse - that's okay"
  - "What can we learn from this?"
  - "Let's get back on track"
  - Update Action Plan based on what happened
  - Don't reset streak immediately (grace period)
- **Relapse Prevention**:
  - "This doesn't have to become a full relapse"
  - "Here's your plan to get back on track"
  - Immediate support resources
  - Sponsor notification (with consent)
- **Learning Opportunity**:
  - "What triggered this?"
  - "What could we do differently?"
  - Update Action Plans

**Implementation**:
- Lapse response system
- Non-judgmental messaging
- Learning-focused approach
- Immediate support
- Action plan updates

**Why It Works**: Non-judgmental response to lapses prevents full relapse.

---

### 23. **Relapse Recovery Plan** ‚≠ê‚≠ê HIGH IMPACT
**Research**: Having a plan for relapse recovery reduces shame and improves outcomes

**How It Works**:
- **If Relapse Occurs**:
  - "You're not alone - many people relapse"
  - "This doesn't mean failure"
  - "Here's your recovery plan"
- **Recovery Steps**:
  1. Get immediate support (call sponsor, go to meeting)
  2. Remove substances/triggers
  3. Update Action Plans
  4. Increase support (more meetings, sponsor contact)
  5. Learn from experience
- **Support Resources**: Treatment centers, hotlines, meetings
- **Non-Judgmental**: Focus on learning and moving forward

**Implementation**:
- Relapse recovery plan (pre-built)
- Step-by-step guidance
- Support resources
- Non-judgmental messaging
- Learning-focused approach

**Why It Works**: Having a plan reduces panic and improves recovery outcomes.
